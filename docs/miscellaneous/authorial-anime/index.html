Authorial Anime

Style CategoryStyle Name (English)Key CharacteristicsArtists/Studios to Reference
Prototypical StyleStudio Ghibli StyleDetailed, lush, hand-painted backgrounds; focus on nature, nostalgia, strong female protagonists, and themes of war/environmentalism.Hayao Miyazaki, Isao Takahata (Spirited Away, My Neighbor Totoro)
Direct InfluenceStudio Ponoc StyleHighly detailed natural environments, traditional animation feel, and focus on whimsy/magic.Studio Ponoc (Mary and the Witch's Flower)
Whimsical/FantasyMamoru Hosoda StyleEmotional depth, mixing fantasy and the modern world, emphasis on family and nature.Studio Chizu (Wolf Children, Belle)
Visual DetailMakoto Shinkai StyleHyper-realistic, vibrant lighting, and stunningly detailed backgrounds, especially of the sky and cities.CoMix Wave Films (Your Name, Weathering with You)
Traditional Anime'90s Anime AestheticSimpler character designs, often hand-drawn quality, focus on natural color palettes, and less digital polish.Kyoto Animation (The Melancholy of Haruhi Suzumiya)
Nature/SceneryAtmospheric Anime LandscapeEmphasis on lush forests, serene waters, and detailed architectural structures, often with a soft, diffused light.Various (Mushishi, A Letter to Momo)

Prototypical Style: Studio Ghibli Style

Direct Influence: Studio Ponoc Style

Whimsical/Fantasy: Mamoru Hosoda Style

Visual Detail: Makoto Shinkai Style

Traditional Anime: '90s Anime Aesthetic

Nature/Scenery: Atmospheric Anime Landscape
